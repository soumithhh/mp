📰 KEY MARKET STORY
US President Trump delivered a national address on the Iran conflict, stopping short of declaring de-escalation and warning of "extremely hard" strikes within 2-3 weeks. This initially sent markets sharply lower (Sensex touched 71,545 intraday), but a RBI-driven rupee surge and IT stock buying drove a strong afternoon recovery. The RBI announced measures to curb speculative currency activity, triggering the rupee's sharpest single-day move in over a decade.

💡 TAKEAWAY
Thursday's session was a classic "volatile recovery" — sharp morning pain followed by a resilient close. The IT sector is clearly the market's anchor right now, with pre-earnings positioning ahead of TCS and Infosys results next week. The rupee stabilization is a positive signal, but Brent near $111/bbl and relentless FII selling (6th straight week of losses for Nifty) means the broader trend remains fragile. With markets closed Friday for Good Friday, the next session (Monday, April 6) will absorb a full weekend's worth of West Asia developments.
